How scoring works

Your score combines three signals:

  • Keyword matching (70%) — We compare your resume against 200+ industry keywords organized into 8 categories (languages, frameworks, databases, DevOps, tools, concepts, education, soft skills), each weighted by importance.
  • Text similarity (20%) — We measure how closely your resume's language matches the job posting overall.
  • Role-specific terms (10%) — We detect important terms unique to this specific posting that aren't in our standard keyword list.

Keywords found in your Skills section carry 1.5× weight. Keywords in Experience carry 1.3×. Keywords in your Summary carry 1.2×.

What this tool measures — and what it doesn't

This tool analyzes keyword overlap and resume structure. It's a good approximation of how automated screening works, but every employer's system is different.

It does not measure: writing quality, career progression, cultural fit, or how a human recruiter would evaluate your experience. Use the score as a guide, not a guarantee.

Common Questions

What is an ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software that employers use to manage job applications. Most large companies use an ATS to automatically screen resumes before a human recruiter ever sees them. These systems typically scan for specific keywords, skills, and qualifications that match the job posting.

What's a good score?

There's no universal "passing" score — it depends on the role and how competitive the applicant pool is. As a general guide:

  • 75%+ — Strong match. Apply with confidence.
  • 60-74% — Worth applying, but tailor your resume first.
  • Below 60% — Significant gaps. Focus on adding missing keywords you genuinely have experience with.
Will a high score guarantee I get an interview?

No. A high score means your resume is well-optimized for automated screening, but hiring decisions involve many factors — experience quality, cover letters, referrals, and more. Think of this tool as removing one barrier, not guaranteeing the outcome.

Is my data safe?

Yes. ATS Scanner runs entirely in your browser. Your resume and job posting text are never sent to any server — all analysis happens locally on your device. The source code is fully open source on GitHub.

Should I add keywords I don't actually have experience with?

No. "Keyword stuffing" — adding skills you don't genuinely have — can backfire badly in interviews. Only add keywords to your resume if you can speak to real experience with them. Focus on honest optimization: making sure the skills you DO have are clearly visible and well-placed.
